General FAQs

 
  1. Who is Switch?  
 
We are a multi-currency savings account that provides customers with added value through an extended partnership network. Our goal is to equip you with the right tools to build a financially healthy future, equip you with the right tools to build a financially healthy lifestyle and to hold your hand through the journey. With Switch, you’re able to address multiple financial needs through a marketplace that gives you the power to make choices on financial products across lending, investments and insurance products.
 
  1. How Is Switch Different From Traditional Banks?
 
Traditional Banks in Nigeria don’t seem to offer any real value anymore, so we Switched it up.
 
Switch is a multi-currency savings account that gives you real choice and control of your finances, in one place, with complete transparency. This account comes with new lending, investment and insurance opportunities, at better rates. The Switch difference is to empower you to plan your future, build wealth and achieve your goals.
 
  1. Who uses Switch?
 
We designed Switch for people who want convenient banking that gives more value - access to loans and income from investments and protection from mishaps with insurance. Switch would also benefit Nigerians in Diaspora who want an easy way to send money home and take advantage of investment opportunities in Nigeria.

  1. Can I Recall A Transaction?
 
Recalling successfully completed bank transfers is possible - but with requirements like an affidavit from the recipient of the transaction, existence of funds in recipient’s account, and recipient’s consent. This means that you may not be able to recall a transaction without contacting us and recalls usually take 3-7 working days, provided the recipient’s consent was gotten. To speed up the process, we encourage our customers to contact us with all the information that they have about the transaction including the account number, time stamp and amount of money transferred. 
 
  1. I am not receiving password reset emails.
 
Please check if the email was received in your "junk" folder. Also make sure that the filters on your email are not contributing to this issue and then try requesting for another message by refilling the “forgot password” form.

Getting Started

 
  1. Using Switch as Your Full-Time Bank
 
Switch is a multi-currency savings account supported by Sterling Bank. This means that we function under the directive of the CBN and funds are insured by the NDIC. We are completely authorized to operate like every other Nigerian Bank. Switch offers you a range of tools that allows you build wealth and closely monitor your finances. Coupled with improved versions of services provided by traditional banks, we provide easy-to-use payment and saving features. Best of all, transaction charges are as limited as possible and cost close to nothing. 
 
  1. How do I Get Started On Switch? 
 
You can register on Switch with these three simple steps. To open an account, simply download the app to your phone and then;
  • Input your personal information and email address
  • Provide a valid phone number that will be verified through an OTP. 
  • Create and verify your pin to protect your account
You are set!

  1. What are the requirements for creating a Switch account?
 
To create a Naira account on Switch, there are a few requirements that are necessary. 
 
  • Personal Information - This includes your name, phone number (with country code), date of birth, gender, nationality and residency status. This information is very important so we encourage customers to double check for errors because access to edit this information is restricted.
  • Bank Verification Number (BVN) - Your bank verification number is a unique eleven digit code authorized by CBN. It can be used by any bank nationwide to identify and verify a customer. If you are having a hard time recalling your BVN, you can dial *565*0# to retrieve it.
  • Valid Identification Card - A valid ID Card usually contains a unique serial number, a photograph of you and your full name. To open a Switch account, you can use an unexpired Driver’s license,Voter’s Card, National ID Card, International Passport, Residency Permit (for Nigerians in Diaspora).
  • Passport Photograph (Photo Selfie) - You are required to upload a close up picture of yourself. This is important to verify your identity and make sure that you match the image on your Identification Card. 
 
Individuals in the diaspora should also provide:
  • Nigerian Contact - This is a relative or friend that resides in Nigeria and can verify your identity. Please provide the name of this person, their phone number, home address and your relationship with them. Validation will take 48 hours.
 
  1. Getting My Switch Number
 
Once you set up your account on Switch, a six-digit code will be displayed under Account details navigated to via the Support icon. it will also be sent to your email address. It can be used to transact with other Switch accounts and is a form of bank identification.  By providing your BVN and verifying your account, your account number will be created and communicated in a similar way to you.
 
  1. Funding My Account
 
As a registered user - a user that has not provided their BVN - you can only fund your wallet through an online payment gateway or through transfers from another Switch wallet. This is because your Switch number will not be recognised as an account number by other banks. However, once you’ve verified your account, you can fund your account by;
  • Online payment gateway - using your card from another bank
  • Bank transfer from another Nigerian bank account 
  • Your account can also be funded through transfers from other Switch accounts.
  • Requesting a payment (with a payment link) from anyone to pay money into your Switch account
 
  1. What kind of account can I open on Switch?
 
You can open a Nigerian Naira Denominated Account, and Domiciliary Accounts (USD, GBP, EUR) will be available soon. Once you’ve validated your account by providing all the necessary requirements, a NGN account is created for you. 
 
  1. Staying Signed In On Multiple Devices
 
For security reasons, we decided to restrict the number of devices you can be signed in on. You will not be able to log into your Switch account on more than one device at a time. You will, also, be required to authorise any device you’re logging in from.
 
  1. Changing My Password On Switch App
 
For safety and security reasons, we encourage our customers to change their password every three months. To do this click on Support and then Account to find the Change Password option. 
 
  1. Can I edit my profile after signup?
 
Your access to your profile is restricted once you sign up, this is to reduce the risk of impersonation and fraudulent activities. However, if you have legal proof of a name change or you have evidence that you changed houses, you can contact our support team to assist you.
 
  1. Deactivating My Account
 
To deactivate your account please send an email to support@switch-ng.com. Deactivating your account will take 2-3 hours. We use this time to unsubscribe you from the various plans you signed up for. However, we shall contact you when there are existing plans that cannot be unsubscribed, and may cause a delay to your account deactivation.

Insurance
 
  1. What classes of insurance are available?
 
Partnering with Switch to provide insurance coverage to our customers, are Reliance HMO, Leadway Assurance and Axa Mansard. Here are a few insurance covers that you will have access to:
  • Vehicle Insurance - Available for private cars, motorcycles, commercial vehicles and fleet insurance. Vehicle Insurance is one of the compulsory insurance classes and anyone using a motor vehicle on the public highway must have it.
  • Life Assurance - A life assurance policy pays a specified sum if the person assured dies, or if they survive a given term of years.
  • Health Insurance - If an insured person has an accident or falls sick, is permanently disabled due to an accident or is unable to work due to accident or sickness. The hospital bills and treatment of the insured person are covered by the insurer. 
  • Travel Insurance - Travel insurance is intended to cover trip cancellation, lost luggage, flight accident and other losses that may occur while travelling.
 
  1. What happens if I fail to make the required premium payment?
 
You pay an annual premium for your insurance plan. Unfortunately, failure to pay the premium means that you may lose your coverage. In cases like vehicle insurance that is legally mandated, failure to pay for premium might restrict your movement. To avoid missing premium payments, you can set it into your saving goals on Switch and follow through by deducting a percentage of the total bill from your account monthly. These terms may slightly differ based on the Insurance Partner you purchase your package from.
 
  1. Can I cancel my insurance plan at any time?
 
Yes, you can. You can cancel your subscription at any point in time. You’ll only be covered as long as your subscription lasts.
 
  1. Are claim services available 24 hours/7 days basis?
 
Yes, claim services are available. Our obligation to you is to be available whenever you need us, especially in an emergency. You can send an email to our support team or reach out directly to your insurer. Either way, we’re committed to providing you with fast claim settlement processes.
 
  1. How long do I have to wait to get my claim resolved?
 
This depends heavily on the claim and the insurer. In the case of a car accident that involves a police report or written statement, it may take up to 30 days to process the claim. In a case of personal injury, the time-frame could range from a few hours to 15 days. 
 
  1. How can I challenge my insurers if they refuse to cover my claims?
 
If your insurer has declined your claims request, and you want to challenge this decision, there are two ways you can seek redress. You can contact the internal dispute resolution panel of whichever partner you’re insured with and request for your claim to be revisited. You can also send a complaint to our support team and after looking into your claim, we can support your appeal to NAICOM against your insurance company. 
 
  1. Are there exclusions or hidden terms I should know about?
 
Yes, there are, however, this is dependent on the company you’re insured with. Some insurance companies offer coverage for motor accidents that occur if the insured is driving while in bad health. Some others charge a small fee on their health coverage if the insured falls ill due to past illnesses and complications. You can reach out to your insurer to find out what exclusions they offer and if there are any hidden terms. 
 
Investment
 
  1. What can I invest in?
 
You can invest in anything from Stocks and Bonds to Treasury Bills to Real Estate to Agriculture. Please join the conversation on our online community to learn of the best investment opportunities in Nigeria and help you decide what options work for you, your personal goals and your risk appetite. There is something for everyone - the trick is knowing what to invest in to serve your goals- we are here to help you figure that out. Further, you can reach out to our support team via email support@switch-ng.com to ask a question.
 
  1. How much interest can I get on my investments?
 
Depending on the investment option and duration of the investment, returns range from 10% to 30% annually. The higher the risk, the higher the return. We’ll guide you every step of the way to ensure you're making the right choices for your growth.
 
  1. How do I determine my risk appetite?
 
Generally, the rule of investments is the higher the risk, the higher the return. Investment products like treasury bills have a low return of about 10% but the risk of loss in products like agricultural investments come with the risk of losing your interest earnings if a loss happens.
 
  1. How soon can I get my money if I decide to liquidate prematurely? Is there a penalty for this? 
 
Depending on the partner you make an investment with, you can access your funds in 1 day at best and within 4 days at worst. However, this action might attract penalties, this also depends on the partner you decide to invest with. 
 
  1. How soon can I get my money if I liquidate at maturity?
 
Once your investment reaches maturity, your account is instantly credited. There is no penalty attached to this. 
 
  1. Can I buy stocks immediately after the registration is complete, even without a CSCS number allocation?
 
 Unfortunately, you can not buy stock without a Central Security Clearing System (CSCS) number. However, a CSCS account will be created for you 48 hours after you have signed up with one of our investment partners offering stock trading. Once this is created, your CSCS number is allocated to you and you can start buying stock and trading. 
 
  1. What’s the time frame for NSE crediting my account?
 
Your account will be credited in 4 working days. All NSE trades get settled in 4 working days. 
 
Loans 
 
  1. Am I eligible for a loan?
 
Yes you are! Anyone who is 21 years and above can apply for a loan. However, approval is at the discretion of the lender.
 
  1. Do I need to pay any fees to apply for a loan?
 
This depends on the partner you’re borrowing from. Some lenders charge an advance fee to cover the costs of processing your application. Please reach out to your lender to find out their practices.
 
  1. Can I delay payment of my loan?
 
Unfortunately, you can not delay the payment of your loan. Payments cannot be delayed because there is an already set payment schedule and a failure to fund your account to accommodate this may cause a negative credit rating.
 
  1. What if I’m late in making a payment?
 
When a loan repayment is due, there’s usually a 7 day grace period (this varies with lender, as some have no grace period). After the grace period, a late payment fee of 2.5% of the month’s repayment is charged every 7 days. 
 
  1. Why was I rejected for a loan?
 
You might be rejected for a loan if:
  • You do not have enough money saved in your bank account to prove that you can make the loan repayments
  • Your credit report includes missed repayments to a loan provider that are more than 14 days past the due date.
  • After considering your income and your existing debts, we think you may struggle to make the loan repayments
 
  1. Is there insurance that will pay off my loans if I lose my source of income, become incapacitated or die?
 
There are a couple of insurance companies that provide life bonuses or credit plans. These are plans that cover your loans if something goes wrong. You can reach out to your insurers to find out if they have one of such plans.
 
  1. What if I don’t have money in my Switch account to make a repayment?
 
You will need to fund your account to make a repayment of your loan. We will send you reminder notifications when your loan tenor is close. We advise our customers to automate monthly deposits into a savings pot for loan repayments.

Technical Requirements

 
  1. What is the minimum operating system required to use Switch?
 
To access the Switch app on an iPhone, you should have 9.1 OS and above installed on your phone. On an Android device, OS 5.0 or later releases should be available on your phone. However, for now, you can only access Switch on any browser on your phone or laptop. 
 
  1. What do I do if I can’t log into my account?
 
It’s not unusual to forget your log in credentials and given that it's possible to get locked out if you keep trying the wrong password, here are some tips to help you regain access to your account:
  • Reset your password - If you find that your current password is not granting you access to your account,  the fastest thing to do is to request to change your password and a link to reset your password will be sent to your email address
  • Clear your cache - Sometimes the reason you can’t get in is that your phone’s memory is full and that has reduced the speed of the phone. To fix this, go to your phone settings and clear your cache, this will free up some space and allow the app to run smoothly.
  • Log in from another browser - It’s possible you’re experiencing a difficult login process because your browser version is outdated. Please update your browser or log in from another.
If everything fails, please contact our support team.
 
 
  1. Connectivity Issues
 
Switch has been designed to perform well even with a bad connection. However, to fully optimize the product, a good connection is important. You can check your internet speed or contact your network provider to make sure that you have a good connection before using Switch. 
 

Security And Device Management

 
  1. How secure is my information?
 
Critical customer data is stored securely. The only data we have access to are -  
  • User ID - Identifying information used to associate each customer to their accounts.
  • Personal Information - Details that cannot be changed by the customer without provision of support documents and confirmation from someone with the appropriate authority (Names, Addresses etc)
  • Permissions - Privileges granted to customers allowing them rights within the app that others do not have (Information that is necessary to switch a user’s access from a wallet to an account)
The data we do not save are -
  • Pins
  • Passwords
  • Account numbers (Other banks)
  • Card details
  • OTPs
Switch as an app is also PCI-DSS level 1 Compliant.
 
  1. How safe is my money?
Switch is a banking startup backed by Sterling Bank, fully approved by CBN and its funds insured by the NDIC to the tune of N500,000.
 
  1. Two Step Authentication
Due to the unique security needs of banks and customers, two-step authentications are usually demanded. Switch requires a two-step authentication for both logging in and for high-value transactions. To set up two-step authentication on the mobile app:
    • Go to Support
    • Click on Security
    • Select the preferred option for Two Factor Authentication and follow the prompts
To set up two-step authentication on our web version:
    • Click on the Profile Image icon on the top right side of the Home Page
    • Click on Security
    • Select the preferred option for Two Factor Authentication and follow the prompts
 
  1. What do I do if I suspect there are unauthorized transactions on my account?
If you suspect irregular activities, you’re encouraged to:
  • Change your password immediately - It is possible that your account has been compromised by someone that has access to your personal information. Resetting your password will make it impossible for the unauthorized entity to make transactions
  • Contact our support team - We encourage our customers to contact us immediately they notice these transactions so that we can temporarily freeze the account while we investigate the issue.
 
  1. How do I distinguish between emails from Switch and other fraudulent emails?
Some scam emails can be very convincing, (especially if they address you personally), but there are a few things to be on the look-out for.
  • Emails from Switch will always be from noreply@switch-ng.com or support@switch-ng.com. If it is from one of our staff it will always have @switch-ng.com as the domain.
  • We will never ask for personal information like your BVN, card details, account number, pin or password. If you get an email asking for any information about your account details or password, please ignore it. 
  • Beware of urgent or threatening language in the email. 
  • Always review the signature at the bottom of each email.
 
  1. What should I do if my phone is stolen?
If you find yourself in a situation where your phone has been stolen or is lost, follow these steps to protect your information on Switch:
  • Contact our support team to report the incident - We’ll keep a closer look at your transaction and temporarily freeze your account if we notice any suspicious activities.
  • Change your password - If you think your account might be compromised, we encourage you to change your password. If you do not have access to a phone to do that, you can log in via our website and change your password from there.
  • Remotely lock and wipe your phone if possible - Both iOS and Android devices have features that help you remotely lock and wipe your phone but you need to activate these features to use them. To do this please contact your service providers or visit their website.
